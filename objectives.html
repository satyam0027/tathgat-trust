Core Objectives

◆

Dedicated to Backward and Underprivileged Communities

Tathagat Trust is specially dedicated to the upliftment of backward and underprivileged communities, with a focused commitment to the Gond, Kol, Bhil, and Vanwasi (Musahar) communities living in presently naxal-affected areas. These communities have endured generations of social exclusion, economic hardship, and limited access to education and skill-based opportunities.

Working at the Frontlines of Exclusion

The Trust operates in Naxal-affected regions of Chhattisgarh, Uttar Pradesh, and Rajasthan, particularly among communities that were historically notified as "criminal" during the British period, resulting in long-standing stigma and marginalisation.

Breaking the Cycle Through Education and Skills

A key objective of Tathagat Trust is to break intergenerational cycles of poverty and exclusion by using education and industry-relevant skill development as tools of de-criminalization, empowerment, and dignity.

Mainstream Integration into Bharat

The Trust aims to integrate backward and underprivileged youth into the social and economic mainstream of Bharat, enabling them to become skilled, self-reliant, and contributing members of society.

Focused Regional & Community Impact

Our initiatives prioritize youth from:

  • Naxal-affected belts of Chhattisgarh
  • Naxal-affected regions of Uttar Pradesh (Kol and Vanwasi communities)
  • Bheel regions of Rajasthan
  • Bawaria communities in Rajasthan, Haryana, and Uttar Pradesh

"In these regions, education is not merely an intervention — it is a lifeline for social justice, stability, and long-term transformation."
